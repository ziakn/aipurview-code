## Verified claims (sampled)
- Claim: "Published in December 2023, it's the first global standard designed specifically for AI governance" (block 1) — ISO 42001:2023 is the first international standard for AI management systems; published by ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42 in December 2023. ✅
- Claim: "ISO 42001 follows the same high-level structure as other ISO management system standards (like ISO 27001)" (block 5) — High-level structure (Plan-Do-Check-Act) is common to ISO MS standards; verified in AIPurview database migrations showing clause numbering alignment (Clauses 4-10 structure mirrors ISO 27001:2022). ✅
- Claim: "The clauses are mandatory, but Annex A is different. You select controls based on your risk assessment" (block 8) — Accurate per ISO 42001 design: Clauses 4-10 are mandatory management system requirements; Annex A controls are reference controls with applicability determination per SoA (Statement of Applicability). ✅
- Claim: "AIPurview helps you build and demonstrate an ISO 42001-compliant AI management system" (block 11) — AIPurview data model includes ISO 42001 framework with clause structures (verified via database migrations 20260420105042, 20260420105045, etc.). Supported features: model inventory, risk management, control mapping, evidence hub, policy management, incident tracking. ✅
- Claim: "Management system clauses" section lists "Seven core clauses required by ISO 42001" with Clauses 4-10 (block 19) — ISO 42001 structure confirmed: Clauses 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10 are the seven mandatory management system clauses. ✅
